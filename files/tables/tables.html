Background

This project was part of my year-long internship that eventually converted into a fulltime design contractor role for Demandbase. All rights for designs belong to Demandbase.

A lot of B2B design is predicated around datatables - presenting information within these tables in a clear, concise and contextual manner is an art of its own. This project was an ongoing undertaking to refresh and rebrand the platform datatables. While a big portion of this project was focused on the visual design of the tables and ensuring that we deliver a slick, elegant solution, an ongoing study was done on improving the usability of the tables, and ensuring the data is clean and human readable. We completed this through user testing and rounds of iteration to deliver an updated and powerful new component that went live in 2022.

Product team: Platform team
Timeline: Q2-Q4 2021
Role: Lead Designer under Principle Designer
Collaborators: Principal Product Manager, Senior UX Researcher, Engineering Lead and UX Design Director for guidance
Tools used: Sketch, InVision, Zoom for UserTesting, DoveTail
Tags: UX Design, UX Research, Interaction design, Data visualization

Problem Statement

The project came about in the form of user feedback:

“The tables in this product are often too cluttered and hard to read, there are confusing click throughs that make it hard to understand where I am in the product, and makes the entire product feel unfinished”

There are quite a few good tidbits to work on here, but I tried to refine it into a central problem statement I could work with.

How might we create powerful, human readable and contextual data tables in our core products?

The hard part of this problem statement was managing the specific needs of the different products of Demandbase. Each product segment had different needs in terms of the type of data being shown, so the tables started to look drastically different in terms of the aesthetic.

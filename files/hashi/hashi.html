HCP Packer
Image Insights

CONTEXT

In the summer of 2022 I interned at HashiCorp. HashiCorp creates DevOps tools that allow develops to build, provision and secure cloud infrastructure. I specifically worked on HCP Packer, which is HashiCorp’s tool to build and provision machine images at scale. Machine images are used in DevOps and IT to segment their end-user groups by function due to the software/OS differences. The finished solution is shown below:

Problem

Practioners need image usage data to inform their machine image lifecycle management at scale. Currently, Packer does not have an easy way to extract this data. We want to provide information contextually to inform the practitioner and aid them in their decision making process.

UX RESEARCH

Lacking context and understanding of this problem space, I set out to investigate by putting together a formal research plan. I started with internal interviews of users of HCP packer to help me understand key terminology workflows, and expectations, before migrating outwards to customers. One method I used to gather rapid qualitative data was to distribute a survey through Reddit. By tapping into HashiCorp’s rich Open source community, I was able to field 70+ responses in 3 days.

PROBLEM DECOMPOSITION

To further understand the problem space and user needs, I decomposed the problem statement to create a clear list of requirements, as well as a how might we statement that I used to start ideating

How Might We...

enable the practitioner to consume image usage insights to inform decision making on their machine image lifecycle management?

DETERMINING METRICS

One of the clear reality was that we had to be picky about which specific insights we would actually start collecting and visualizing. Part of that was deciding what actually is being collected right now, and what could be exposed.

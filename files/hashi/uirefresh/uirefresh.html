HCP Terraform
UI Refresh

Context

Developer tools are notoriously known for having poor user interfaces, its like the field is allergic to products that are both powerful and also nice to look at üòù. So you can imagine my excitement when I was tapped to work on some UI improvements for HCP Terraform, a managed service to deploy and track cloud infrastructure. During my time at HashiCorp, I had often focused more on feature work, but I am a deep proponent of continuous improvement of the overall experience. This project was a key lesson in managing scope creep and taking an ambiguous project brief and turning it into something of value

When: Q2 2024-Q1 2025

Team: Product Designer (Me), Eng Lead,

Detailed Case Study

The Brief

Improve the UI of some of the high traffic pages of our application.

Where to start?

As a designer, your eyes will often roll into the back of your head at the sheer utterance of ‚Äúmake this look nicer‚Äù, or ‚Äúmake it pop‚Äù, but this was the rare occasion in which I was really happy to hear this being mentioned. The UI of Terraform Cloud had gotten incremental improvements over time, but hadn‚Äôt gotten a true facelift since its inception in 2018. A lot can change in 6 years, and this was no exception.

The core problem that a lot of SaaS companies face is the incessant need for new features to prevent churn and bring in new contracts. There often feels like a push and pull between reducing tech and design debt, and bringing new features that make customers excited. Unfortunately, due to this reason we lacked complete buyin company wide that this was important to work on. This project had executive sponsorship via design and engineering, but didn‚Äôt get product buy in. This was highlighted by the EPD Structure of this project, in which we lacked true product management ownership.

This fuzzy ownership model was a key theme in this project, so remember that going forward.

Rooting redesigns in real problems

I wanted to root all the potential UI changes that I was proposing in real customer problems, and less gut feeling. To do this I started out with understanding sentiment in product through two main methods: cognitive ethnography and direct feedback form.

External foundation setting

Cognitive ethnography was done through social media, I deemed this method suitable for 2 core reasons, time and cost. Tapping into HashiCorp‚Äôs rich user base was easy and free, and could help set a foundation for what to optimize on.

Compounding this, I gathered some adhoc direct feedback through anonymous surveys on reddit and in-app feedback forms that were housed on snowflake.

This set an initial foundation for what to look out for and improve.

Key Learning

Users find significant friction in high traffic pages that don‚Äôt meet basic usability goals and force users to complete extra steps to consume data.

Additionally, I leaned on understanding what design patterns that practitioners are familiar with and what could be brought over from that. By offloading the need to re-learn the UI, I could make the new UI more efficient.

Internal foundation setting

The next step was to engage with the internal liaison for customers - the field and PM‚Äôs. Both levels of subject matter experts could help point me towards larger pain points with the UI that had been collected but perhaps not prioritized. This could help us to justify working on this project. From here, I created a simple affinity map, and then tailored my first attempt at ideating on what an improvement could look like.

Key Learning

High traffic pages that the users land on influence brand perception, and in their current state don‚Äôt fit into the more evolved design language of newer features.

Key Learning

In order to get internal buy in, we must manage scope creep and provide clear foundational wins to the organization.

Design goal

Deliver a highly scoped redesign of the 4 highest traffic pages on terraform to improve usability, accelerate design system adoption, and create a more recognizable design language without introducing new features.
